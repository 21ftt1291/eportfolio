Pekan Tutong

Mukim Pekan Tutong is a mukim in Tutong District, Brunei. The population was 10,551 in 2016.[1] It encompasses Pekan Tutong, the district's sole town and administrative centre.

The mukim is located in the north-west of Tutong District, bordering the South China Sea to the north-west, Mukim Keriam to the east, Mukim Kiudang to the south-east, Mukim Tanjong Maya to the south and Mukim Telisai to the south-west.[citation needed] The Tutong River runs its course through the mukim and flows out into South China Sea. There are a few islands along the river, among them the islets of Setawat (Pulau Setawat) and Bakuku (Pulau Bakuku).
